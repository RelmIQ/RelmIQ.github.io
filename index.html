Why it matters

The questions your
dashboard isn't answering.

These are the blind spots we fix for every client.

Attribution
Meta says it drove £50k. Google says £40k. Email says £35k. Total: £125k. Actual revenue: £80k.
Every platform over-claims attribution. Without a single de-duplicated model, your budget decisions are based on whoever shouts loudest — not what actually drives revenue.
"If you add up what your channels each claim they drove last month, does it match your actual revenue? It never does."
Margin
Your best ROAS campaign might have a 40% return rate quietly destroying your margin.
Meta's dashboard fires a conversion when someone checks out. It has no idea what came back a week later. That data lives in Shopify — and the two systems never talk.
"Your ROAS looks healthy. But do you know the return rate on each campaign? That gap is where the margin goes."
Customer value
Most brands optimise for the cheapest first purchase. The ones that scale optimise for lifetime value.
Not all acquisition channels are equal. Some bring in one-time buyers. Others bring in customers who spend 3x more over 12 months. You can't see this without connecting your ad data to your customer data.
"Which of your acquisition channels brings in customers who come back? Most brands don't know — and it changes everything."
Buying decisions
Next season's buy is based on last year's gut feel. A sell-through dashboard changes that.
What was your sell-through rate on outerwear last AW versus SS? If your buyer can't answer that in 30 seconds, the decisions for next season are based on memory — not data.
"Dead stock is a data problem. We turn it into a buying decision."

Womenswear — scaling brand
From 5 spreadsheets to one dashboard
A UK womenswear brand doing £2.3m revenue had five different people maintaining five different spreadsheets — none of which agreed with each other. We connected Shopify, Meta Ads, GA4, and Klaviyo into a single Metabase dashboard. Weekly reporting time dropped from 6 hours to zero. The team discovered their highest-LTV customers were acquired via email, not paid social — and reallocated £8k/month in budget accordingly.

Accessories — growth stage
ROAS looked great. Margin didn't.
A accessories brand was scaling their best-performing Meta campaign — 4.8x ROAS, strong by any measure. Our audit connected campaign data to Shopify returns data and found a 38% return rate on that specific campaign. Net ROAS after returns: 2.1x. The campaign targeting bestsellers with an 8% return rate and 3.2x ROAS was actually the stronger performer. Budget was reallocated in week one.
